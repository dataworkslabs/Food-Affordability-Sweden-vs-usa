Food Affordability Analysis: U.S. vs Sweden
Overview

Food prices have become a political and economic pressure point in both the United States and Sweden. Rising grocery costs, uneven wage growth, and inflation have contributed to a growing concern:

Is eating a healthy diet still affordable?

This project analyzes the cost of healthy eating across both countries, combining international datasets with real-world pricing data to better understand affordability across income levels.

Key Questions
How has the cost of a healthy diet changed from 2017–2024?
How do the United States and Sweden compare at a national level?
Do official datasets accurately reflect real-world food costs?
How does affordability differ across income groups?
Is “healthy eating” becoming inaccessible for lower-income populations?
